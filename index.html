Approved Treatments at Delta Plus Clinics

1. Dry Cupping Therapy

Purpose: Address muscle and bone pain, improve blood flow, and promote relaxation.

Procedure: Place suction cups on the skin to create negative pressure, which may be left stationary or moved.

Risks: Temporary redness or bruising, mild pain; rarely, skin irritation or burns.

Benefits: May reduce pain, improve mobility, and enhance relaxation; results vary by individual.

2. Cold Chamber Therapy

Purpose: Reduce inflammation, relieve pain, and enhance recovery using extreme cold.

Procedure: Exposure to sub-zero temperatures in a controlled chamber for a short duration.

Risks: Temporary discomfort, skin redness, or shivering; rarely, frostbite or respiratory issues.

Benefits: May reduce inflammation, improve recovery, and alleviate pain; results vary by individual.

3. Dry Needling Therapy

Purpose: Relieve muscle pain and improve musculoskeletal function by targeting trigger points.

Procedure: Insert sterile, single-use needles into muscle trigger points to stimulate a twitch response.

Risks: Temporary pain or bruising, temporary symptom exacerbation; rarely, infection or injury.

Benefits: May reduce pain, improve range of motion, and enhance function; results vary by individual.

4. Lymphatic Drainage Therapy

Purpose: Reduce swelling, improve lymphatic circulation, and promote detoxification.

Procedure: Gentle, rhythmic massage to stimulate lymphatic vessels and enhance lymph fluid flow.

Risks: Temporary soreness or sensitivity, mild discomfort; rarely, allergic reactions to massage oils.

Benefits: May reduce swelling, improve immune function, and enhance well-being; results vary by individual.

5. Gait Analysis

Purpose: Assess walking patterns and biomechanics of movement to plan treatment.

Procedure: Observe and analyze gait using visual inspection, video, or sensor devices.

Risks: Minimal; may include mild discomfort during walking tests.

Benefits: Provides data to develop targeted treatment plans; outcomes depend on subsequent treatment.

6. Zero Gravity Machine Therapy

Purpose: Facilitate exercises with partial weight-bearing and gait training for rehabilitation.

Procedure: Use a machine to reduce body weight during exercise, allowing controlled movement.

Risks: Mild discomfort or muscle soreness; rarely, equipment-related issues.

Benefits: May improve mobility and strength while reducing joint stress; results vary.

7. Aquatic Therapy

Purpose: Enhance mobility, reduce pain, and promote rehabilitation using water-based exercises.

Procedure: Supervised exercises in a warm water pool to leverage buoyancy and resistance.

Risks: Temporary muscle soreness, slips, or skin irritation; rarely, water-related infections.

Benefits: May improve mobility, reduce pain, and enhance strength; results vary.

8. Shockwave Therapy

Purpose: Treat chronic pain and promote tissue healing using sound waves.

Procedure: Apply high-energy sound waves to affected areas using a handheld device.

Risks: Temporary pain, redness, or bruising; rarely, tissue irritation.

Benefits: May reduce pain and promote healing; results vary by individual.

9. Laser Therapy

Purpose: Reduce pain and inflammation and promote tissue healing using low-level laser light.

Procedure: Apply a handheld laser device to emit light energy to affected tissues.

Risks: Mild warmth, tingling, or redness; rarely, symptom exacerbation or allergic reactions.

Benefits: May relieve pain, accelerate healing, and improve mobility; results vary.

10. Electrical Therapy

Purpose: Manage pain, stimulate muscle contraction, and promote tissue healing using electrical currents.

Procedure: Apply electrodes to deliver controlled electrical pulses to affected areas.

Risks: Mild discomfort, skin irritation; rarely, burns or muscle soreness.

Benefits: May reduce pain, improve muscle function, and aid healing; results vary.

11. Pediatric Physical Therapy (if applicable)

Purpose: Address developmental delays, disabilities, injuries, or conditions affecting a child’s overall health and functional abilities.

Procedure: Therapeutic sessions including activities, exercises, and interventions tailored to the child’s needs in a safe, supportive environment, potentially including physical therapy (to improve gross motor skills), occupational therapy (to enhance fine motor skills), or speech therapy (to address communication delays).

Risks: Temporary discomfort or frustration during sessions; rarely, allergic reactions to equipment or materials.

Benefits: Improved motor skills, coordination, daily abilities, or communication functions; results vary by child.

12. Therapy by Opposite-Gender Specialist

Purpose: Provide physical therapy services by an opposite-gender specialist due to their expertise, ensuring optimal care continuity.

Procedure: Perform selected treatments by a licensed opposite-gender specialist, with prior coordination and consent, adhering to professional and ethical standards.

Risks: No direct risks related to gender, though some may feel personal discomfort.

Benefits: Ensures treatment continuity regardless of specialist gender, offering diverse expertise and perspectives to enhance comprehensive care and health well-being.
